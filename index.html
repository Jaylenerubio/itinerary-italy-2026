Venice

Jun 13–15

📍 Hyatt Centric · Murano Island

Sat, Jun 13 Arrival evening — Murano
⛵
~8:00 PM
Arrive — vaporetto to Murano
From Santa Lucia station, take vaporetto Line 4.1 or 4.2 directly to Murano (~45 min, €9.50 pp). Or splurge on a private water taxi (~€80–100 for the group) for a more dramatic arrival. Note: gondolas don't travel to Murano.
💡 Buy a 24-hr vaporetto pass (€25) — use it all day Sunday too
🌙
Dinner — La Fornace Murano
Fondamenta Manin, 1 · 4.7 ⭐ · Gorgeous waterside restaurant with Murano glass on every table. Reviewers rave about the octopus and shrimp pasta. Ask about GF pasta options when booking.
📋 Reserve ahead
Sun, Jun 14 Venice proper + Murano afternoon
☕
Morning
Breakfast at Hyatt or a Murano café
Check if hotel breakfast is included. Otherwise, grab a cornetto and espresso standing at a local bar — the Italian way, and usually cheaper.
⛪
Morning
Vaporetto to Venice — St. Mark's Basilica
Line 4.1/4.2 to Fondamente Nove, then Line 1 to San Marco. Arrive before 11 AM to beat the crowds. Free to enter — climb to the loggia for views over the Piazza. The golden mosaics inside are breathtaking.
💡 Book the Pala d'Oro skip-the-line online
🚶
Wander — Cannaregio or Dorsoduro
Get gloriously lost after St. Mark's. Head toward Rialto Bridge for photos, then duck into quieter streets. Dorsoduro has the Peggy Guggenheim Collection if you want an art detour.
🍽️
Lunch
Lunch in Venice — La Piazza or Osteria Da Bubi
La Piazza (S. Marco 615 · 4.7 ⭐) — lobster pasta and seafood are standouts, right by St. Mark's. Or Osteria Da Bubi (Castello 5306 · 4.7 ⭐) for the legendary truffle fettuccine and octopus. Both GF-accommodating.
📋 Reserve ahead for weekend lunch
🪟
Afternoon
Return to Murano — glass factories
Murano is world-famous for its glass. Visit the Museo del Vetro and watch a free live glassblowing demo at one of the fornaci. Perfect for authentic souvenirs — Fornace Mian and Davide Fuin are the real deal.
💡 Avoid tourist-trap glass near main vaporetto stops
🌙
Dinner — Osteria Al Duomo or La Fornace
Osteria Al Duomo (Fond. Navagero 20/21 · 4.3 ⭐) — Murano's biggest trattoria with a lovely garden, great for a group. The duck ragù spaghetti is a standout. GF options available.

🌿 GF/DF tips for Italy: Always say "senza glutine" (gluten-free) and "senza lattosio" (dairy-free) when ordering. Most Italian kitchens accommodate when asked clearly — especially for pasta, risotto, and grilled proteins. For gelato, look for sorbetto (dairy-free) or ask which flavors are made with fruit and water. Gelateria dei Gracchi in Rome has confirmed GF + DF options and is a must-visit.
